Refuse & Report: Breaking the Chain

Topic: Take Action Pillar

Every bribe offered or accepted is a link in the chain of corruption. The first step is to **refuse** to participate. The second, more difficult step, is to **report** it.

Reporting corruption is not just a civic duty; it is an act of profound courage. This page provides the official, real channels where you can report corruption related to Central Government officials.

How to Report to the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC)

The CVC is India's apex integrity institution. It handles complaints of corruption against central government employees, PSUs, and banks. (For state government employees, you must contact your state's Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB)).

CVC Online Portal

The most effective way. Visit portal.cvc.gov.in to lodge a detailed complaint. You can track its status online.

Toll-Free Helpline

Call 1800-11-0180 or 1964 to report a demand for a bribe or other corruption-related issues.

"VIGEYE" App

Download the CVC's mobile app ("Vigilance Eye") to report corruption, attach evidence, and track your complaint.

What About Whistleblower Protection?

The fear of reprisal is the main reason people stay silent. To combat this, India has the **Public Interest Disclosure and Protection of Informers (PIDPI) Resolution, 2004**, also known as the **Whistle Blowers Protection Act**.

Under this act, the CVC is the designated agency to receive complaints from whistleblowers. If you are a whistleblower, you can lodge a complaint directly with the Secretary, CVC, in a sealed envelope marked "Complaint under PIDPI." The CVC is mandated to keep your identity **confidential**.

This protection is a direct result of the sacrifices of heroes like **Satyendra Dubey** and **Shanmugam Manjunath**, who were murdered for exposing corruption and whose stories we honor on our 'Integrity' page.
