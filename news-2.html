Civil Society Celebrates 20 Years of the RTI Act

Published on October 28, 2025 | By Campaign Staff

This month marks the 20th anniversary of the Right to Information (RTI) Act, which came into force on October 12, 2005. This landmark legislation has fundamentally altered the relationship between the citizen and the state, moving India from a culture of official secrecy to one of transparency and accountability.

Born from a grassroots movement, the RTI Act empowers any Indian citizen to request information from a public authority. It has become a vital tool for exposing corruption, uncovering wrongdoing, and demanding answers. From rural villages to the highest offices in New Delhi, the RTI has been used to hold power to account.

The Impact of RTI (2005-2025)

The Act's power is best measured by its use. In its first 10 years alone, its impact was revolutionary, leading to major exposures and systemic changes.

Election Reform

Landmark SC cases (like ADR vs. UoI) used RTI principles to force candidates to disclose criminal records

Future Challenges

While we celebrate these successes, the RTI Act faces serious challenges. A growing backlog of appeals, which has crossed over 4 lakh cases, threatens to delay justice. Furthermore, there are ongoing concerns about the dilution of the Act's provisions and the safety of RTI activists.

As we mark 20 years, we must not only celebrate but also pledge to protect this fundamental right. The fight for transparency is far from over, and the RTI Act remains our most powerful weapon.
