National Anti-Corruption Channels: How to Report

Published on October 25, 2025 | By Campaign Staff

A critical part of fighting corruption is empowering citizens to report it safely and effectively. While our campaign raises awareness, it's vital to know the official government channels designed to handle such complaints. The Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) is the apex integrity institution in India.

The CVC has established multiple channels for citizens to file complaints regarding corruption by Central Government officials. It is crucial to use these official channels to ensure your complaint is registered and investigated.

Helpline & Complaint Channels

You can report corruption using any of the following methods. These systems are designed to be accessible and, in many cases, to protect your identity.

1800-11-0180

CVC Toll-Free Helpline Number for lodging complaints

CVC Online Portal

Lodge a complaint directly via the official portal: portal.cvc.gov.in

"VIGEYE" App

Download the CVC's official mobile app to report corruption

What You Should Know

These channels are specifically for complaints against **Central Government employees, Public Sector Banks, Public Sector Undertakings, and other central bodies.** For corruption by state government employees, you must contact your state's Vigilance or Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB).

Furthermore, the CVC operates under the **Public Interest Disclosure and Protection of Informers (PIDPI) Resolution**, also known as the Whistleblower Resolution. If you are reporting as a whistleblower and fear for your safety, you can request protection under this resolution, and the CVC is mandated to keep your identity confidential.
