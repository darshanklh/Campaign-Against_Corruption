Demand Your Rights: A Guide to the RTI Act

Topic: Take Action Pillar

The **Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005** is your single most powerful legal tool to demand transparency. It allows any Indian citizen to request information from any public authority (government body).

You can ask for anything from "How much money was spent on the road in my neighborhood?" to "What are the attendance records of my local school's teachers?" This right is fundamental to holding officials accountable.

How to File an RTI Application (Online)

For central government departments, the process is incredibly simple via the `rtionline.gov.in` portal.

  1. Visit the Portal: Go to rtionline.gov.in and click "Submit Request."
  2. Select Authority: Choose the Ministry or Department you want to get information from (e.g., Ministry of Road Transport, PMO, etc.).
  3. Enter Details: Fill in your personal details. You are only required to provide your name and address.
  4. Write Your Query: In the text box, ask your questions. Be clear, specific, and direct.
    • Bad Question: "Tell me about corruption in the passport office." (Too vague)
    • Good Question: "Please provide a daily log of all passport applications marked 'delayed' at the XYZ Passport Office between Jan 1, 2025, and Feb 1, 2025, and the specific reason recorded for each delay."
  5. Pay the Fee: Pay the ₹10 application fee online.
  6. Submit & Save: You will get a registration number. Save it to track your application.

By law, the public authority **must respond within 30 days**. If they don't, or if you are unhappy with the answer, you can file a "First Appeal" on the same portal, for free.

₹10

The standard application fee to file an RTI.

30 Days

The legal deadline for a Public Information Officer (PIO) to respond to your query.

Pending appeals and complaints across 29 Information Commissions in India, a major challenge to the Act.
