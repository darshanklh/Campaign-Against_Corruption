Educate & Aware: The First Step to Change

Topic: Take Action Pillar

Corruption thrives in silence, on the belief that "this is just how things are done." The single most powerful tool to fight this is **awareness**. An educated and aware public is the corrupt's worst enemy.

The Global Picture: Where Does India Stand?

Transparency International, a global anti-corruption organization, publishes the **Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI)** annually. It scores and ranks countries based on how corrupt their public sector is perceived to be by experts and businesspeople.

This index is a crucial tool for awareness. It gives us a clear, data-backed metric to measure our progress (or lack thereof) against the rest of the world.

93 / 180

India's rank in the 2023 Corruption Perceptions Index (out of 180 countries).

39 / 100

India's score, where 100 is "very clean" and 0 is "highly corrupt."

45%

Percentage of people in Asia who think corruption has worsened in their country (2023 Global Corruption Barometer).

What You Can Do

Awareness starts at home. It's not just about sharing news, but about changing conversations.

  • Talk to Your Family: Discuss the difference between a "gift" and a "bribe." Explain to younger generations why paying a bribe for a service (like a passport or license) isn't a "shortcut," but a crime that hurts everyone.
  • Understand Your Rights: You cannot be an aware citizen if you don't know your rights. Learn about the RTI Act, the Consumer Protection Act, and your rights when dealing with the police.
  • Share Real Stories: Use social media to share the success stories you read on our "Stories" page. Show people that change is possible and that individuals can make a difference.

When an entire community expects transparency, officials are forced to provide it. Your voice is the first domino to fall.
