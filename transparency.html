Promoting Transparency: The Best Disinfectant

Topic: Our Core Mission Pillar

Transparency is the most powerful antidote to corruption. When government actions are open to public scrutiny, it becomes exponentially harder for malfeasance to hide. Our campaign focuses on strengthening and utilizing two key pillars of transparency in India: **The Right to Information (RTI) Act** and **Digital Governance Portals**.

Case Study 1: The Right to Information (RTI) Act, 2005

The RTI Act is arguably the most significant legislation empowering Indian citizens. It allows any citizen to request information from a public authority, turning the tables on a culture of secrecy. Its impact has been revolutionary.

Landmark scams exposed or detailed by the RTI Act include:

  • The Adarsh Housing Society Scam: RTI applications revealed how a building meant for war widows was illegally allotted to politicians, bureaucrats, and military officials.
  • The Vyapam Scam: RTI queries exposed a massive admissions and recruitment scam in Madhya Pradesh, involving manipulated exams and impersonations.
  • Commonwealth Games (CWG) Scam: Activists used RTI to uncover gross financial irregularities and inflated contracts in the 2010 Delhi games.

Case Study 2: Government e-Marketplace (GeM)

Petty corruption often thrives in government procurement—the process of buying goods and services. The **Government e-Marketplace (GeM)** portal was launched to combat this by creating a single, open, and transparent online platform for all government purchasing.

₹40,000 Crore+

Total estimated savings for the government due to transparent pricing and open bidding (as of 2023).

6 Million+

Sellers and service providers, including a vast number of small (MSME) businesses, now have direct access to government tenders.

100% Paperless

The entire process, from bid to payment, is digital, creating a clear audit trail and reducing opportunities for bribery.

Our Goal

Our mission is to encourage every citizen to **use the RTI Act** to question public authorities. We also advocate for expanding transparent digital platforms like GeM to all areas of government spending, from municipal contracts to welfare distribution. When every rupee is accounted for in public, corruption cannot survive in the shadows.
