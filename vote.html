Vote Responsibly: Clean Politics is a Choice

Topic: Take Action Pillar

A government free of corruption begins long before anyone takes office. It begins at the ballot box. Voting responsibly means researching your candidates and rejecting those with a history of corruption or criminal charges.

The **Association for Democratic Reforms (ADR)** is a non-partisan organization that meticulously analyzes the self-sworn affidavits of election candidates. Their findings are a critical tool for every voter.

The Data: Criminalization of Politics

The "graph" of criminalization in our politics is a rising line. According to ADR reports, the percentage of Members of Parliament (MPs) in the Lok Sabha with declared criminal cases against them has been steadily increasing.

34% (2014)

Percentage of winning MPs in the 2014 Lok Sabha with declared criminal cases.

43% (2019)

Percentage of winning MPs in the 2019 Lok Sabha with declared criminal cases.

46% (2024)

Percentage of winning MPs in the 2024 Lok Sabha with declared criminal cases (251 of 543).

How to Check Your Candidate

You have the power to check the records before you vote. Do not rely on hearsay or campaign slogans. Use these tools:

  • ADR / MyNeta: Visit myneta.info. You can search by constituency or candidate name to see their full affidavit, including criminal cases, financial assets, and liabilities.
  • ECI "Know Your Candidate" App: The Election Commission of India (ECI) has a mobile app called **"KYC"** that requires political parties to publish the criminal antecedents of their candidates.

When voters prove that they will not elect candidates with criminal records, parties will be forced to nominate clean candidates. This is the most direct way to ensure long-term, systemic change.
